  • Reference and link to other essay on the promise

  • But if it's so amazing, why hasn't it been successful?

  • Let's try to predict the future
  • Challenges fall into two major categories:
    • Difficulties in the Devices Themselves
    • Difficulties Posed by the External World

DEVICES THEMSELVES

Mobile

  • Might be appropriate sometimes, but use of hands and screen is not immersive
  • May not be better than just a screen / watch / etc.

FoV

  • Most common complaint when people put on Hololens for the first time
  • Not necessarily like a low-resolution monitor - it actually means that objects disappear as you move around
  • Real objects don't behave that way. And it might have downstream effects---LIMITS THE STAGE

THE VERGENCE-ACCOMODATION CONFLICT

  • Much bigger problem, but a lot more nuanced
  • Technically complicated, but the result is that there's a limited space around the user where you can put virtual objects
  • Is an INTRINSIC PROPERTY of stereoscopic displays. CANNOT be resolved incrementally, would require ENTIRELY NEW KIND of technology to resovle
  • Further Limits the Stage
  • IMPORTANTLY: Removes the possibility of hands-reach objects
  • Doesn't mean it's IMPOSSIBLE to place things there, just INADVISABLE and especially bad for long-term comfort
  • Does mean that it's IMPOSSIBLE TO DUAL FOCUS
  • No surgery. No maintenance. No nothing.

SIZE, WEIGHT, BATTERY

  • Uncomfortable
  • No Moore's Law
  • Certain fundamental physical limits

IMAGE QUALITY

  • Not always a dealbreaker, but has impacts
  • Why use shitty virtual monitor when a real one exists?
  • Additive light kinda sucks, no shadows, no blacks, not realistic

LOGISTICS / ENVIRONMENT / USE CASES

ENVIRONMENT

  • Algorithms for placing and tracking objects in space are impressive
  • But they're real, which means they have real constraints
  • Some applications require more precision than possible
  • Some objects look identical, so CV algorithms could never handle it
  • Making sense of unstructured environments is very fucking hard
  • Controlling objects requires controllable objects, so it's not worthwhile

THE GLASSHOLE EFFECT

  • Entirely possible that these would really be that good, in a platonic sense
  • But when push comes to shove people just don't want them, don't warn to them
  • Strikes me as less likely (given enough benefits, people will do almost anything)
  • Yet always possible

MAYBE IT JUST INS'T ALL THAT VALUABLE

  • In another essay, I tried to lay out the major categories of usefulness for spatial computing
  • But there, I didn't really consider the possibility that maybe these just aren't all that compelling
  • Entirely possible for the technology to work, but for it to just not be that useful
  • Maybe I'm overestimating how much help we need in navigating the world. Or maybe we don't need info from objects
  • Maybe the singularity happens first

CLOSING THOUGHTS

  • Challenges are very real, and perhaps insurmountable
  • Clearly limit what's possible today. Might limit what's possible forever
  • But there's still some reason to be excited, if not exactly optimistic
  • Certain things are possible, so worth exploring
  • Always possible for black swans
  • End with same message of taking things on a case-by-case basis
